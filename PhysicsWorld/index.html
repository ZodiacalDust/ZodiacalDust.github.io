‚ÄúAll stars, including our Sun, form in the immense stellar nurseries that we call nebulae, which we may regard as reservoirs of gas and dust..." -- Brian May

This might be a bit surprising but... Brian May, my rock n' roll hero, is actually the first Astronomer leading me into Astrophysics.
I bought an Astronomy book written by him from a flea market, when I was a little little little kid!
The book is called "Bang! The Complete History of the Universe". And my favorite chapter is "Chapter 4 Stars and Planets".
Brian explains how small dust partcles accrete to form the stars and planets. He did it clearly and intriguingly.
And to me at that time, knowing that the huge stars and exoplanets are actually made by the tiny dust particles was incredible...that's how I found Astronomy fun!
