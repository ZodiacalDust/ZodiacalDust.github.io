Why do we see the world in a stereo view? Have you ever thought about that?
Well, this is because of Parallex

Stretch your hand with your thumb in front of sth. Like what this graph shows to you. Keep your thumb staying at the same position.
Close your left eye, use only your right eye to look at your thumb; then try the opposite way - use your left eye only.
You will find the position of your thumb differs, so surprising!
This is what the strange phenomenon is: 'Parallax is the effect of changing position of an object when viewed from different positions'. -- quoted from Oxford Dictionary.

See? The magic of parallax!!!

Put you hand back, and look at the draft graph above again.
Cross your eyes, then you will find the 'left view' and 'right view' of 'the block and the thumb' are overlapping.
You can see a stereo image of 'the block and the thumb' now! Try it !!!

Yes, this is cross-eye method to view Stereoscopics.
